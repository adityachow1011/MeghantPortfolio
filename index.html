PROJECT TOPIC-VIRTUAL SCREENING OF PHYTOCHEMICALS TO HIGHLIGHT THE HIT MOLECULES FOR SKIN LIGHTENING FORMULATIONS
Under the guidance of
Dr. Piyali Basak
Associate Professor
School of Bioscience and Engineering
Jadavpur University Kolkata-700032
Project Steps:

1. Article and Compound Selection:-I selected a research article focusing on virtual screening and a standard compound, Hesperidin, to guide my study.

2. Proteins and Ligands Selection:-Three proteins associated with skin lightening—Tyrosinase, Elastase, and Collagenase—were chosen based on literature. Ligands were randomly selected from databases like IMPPAT, PubChem, and RCSB, adhering to Lipinski's rule.
3. Designing and Cleaning:-Utilizing tools such as Discovery Studio, Chimera, Open Babel, and Autodock Vienna, I designed and cleaned ligands and proteins to ensure accurate virtual screening.

4. Virtual Screening for Skin Lightening:-The virtual screening aimed to identify potential hit molecules among the selected ligands for skin lightening formulations.

5. Comparison with Standard Compound:-Finally, I compared the outcomes of the virtual screening with the standard skin lightening compound, Hesperidin, to assess the effectiveness of the selected ligands.

Project Significance: This project focussed into virtual screening of phytochemicals for developing skin lightening formulations. By identifying hit molecules through virtual screening and comparing them with a standard compound, Hesperidin, the study contributes valuable insights to the field of cosmetic and therapeutic research. The project's main goal was to spotlight potential candidates for skin lightening formulations through a comprehensive virtual screening approach.
